The Black Hole

You're floating further and further until you feel something pulling you down. Your body heavy, your body getting pulled in. You look up to notice it's a black hole. A big one. Time seems to slow, you see clocks flying past. You close your eyes and take a deep breathe in. You let it take you in.

Depression

According to the World Health Organization, depression is "persistent sadness and a lack of interest or pleasure from previously rewarding or enjoyable activities,” and globally, about 5% of adults suffer from depression."

Depression has become a common term to refer to prolonged sadness in media, but that in itself may undermine the reality of those who experience depression as a condition. There are different types of depression such as major depression, seasonal affective disorder, and bipolar disorder. Each has its own set of longetivity and extent of symptoms. It is important to see depression as a condition that can range from different extremities, so not all people will look exactly the same.

Common Symptoms

According to the National Institute of Mental Health, symptoms of depression include (but not all):

Persistent sad, anxious, "empty" mood
Feeling hopeless or pessimistic
Feelings of irritability, frustration, restelessness
Lost of Interest in what you normally enjoyed (hobbies, activities)
Decreased energy, fatiugue, inability to concentrate or remember
Change in appetite
Physical aches
Perceived slower sense of time

Statistics

Data from the American College Health Association (ACHA) shows t27% of college students noted having depression in 2022, with 78% reporting that they reached out to a healthcare professional. A major factor that contributed to this statistic was COVID, which began in 2020 and is ongoing today. Having to be isolated for so long without social support has taken a toll on mental health, especially among young adults.

Common Strategies to Cope

Eat healthy, nutritious food
Exercise daily, go outside for fresh air
Reach out to your close circle, spend time with loved ones
Reach out to a professional in regards to medication

Inside the black hole, you feel as if you're being stretched to pieces. You feel you can't escape until a hand reaches out to you. You immediately grab it and you're pulled out. Who was your savior? It's unclear, as no one is around as you're back into space, floating once again. Filled with panic, you take a deep breathe in and calm your body. You float further once again.
