The Burnt Out Star

Welcome to the Supernova, or more, "the Burnt Out Star." The supernova occurs when a star is dying, with it's fire inside fading and dimming as time passes until it will explode. As you float close to the star, you notice your body feeling heavier. You connect with the star.

Burnout

The feeling of burnout is a common feeling amongst all of us, especially as students. Often times we wonder, "How did I become like this? I was so strongheaded and organized not too long ago, what's wrong with me?" It occurs after a prolonged amount of stress that gets to the point of interfering with our ability to do daily tasks.

Common signs of Burnout are:

As per Malvern Behavioral Health, Jerry Kosmin refers to these signs of burnout:

ExhaustionEven with a lot of sleep, you feel as if you can't go about with your day without feeling fatigue.
Lack of Motivation and InterestYou feel diminished feelings of doing daily tasks, or things you used to enjoy.
Lack of Creativity and ConcentrationWith fatigue comes a decrease in our creative abilities, leading to prolongued procrastination. You feel unable to feel organized or motivated to do work.
Increased IrritabilityYou get to the point where you get mad at yourself for being incapable of doing what you normally were able to do. It's not your fault, but you feel as it is. 
Frequent IllnessesAfter prolonged stress and overdoing yourself, your body will start to wear out and tell you it needs a break. Not taking a break and pushing yourself will wear you out even further.

With increased stress is a higher toll on your body, leading to high cortisol (stress hormone) levels, high blood pressure, and ultimately fatigue. Most common factors that contribute to such stress in students is comparing ourselves to other people, immense workloads, neverending deadlines, and feelings of loneliness.  Anxiety is all over your mind, you feel susceptible to be depressed. You can't help but wonder if you're even doing the right thing.

Did you know loneliness is as deadly as smoking 15 cigarettes a day?

There is such a thing as "good stress" and "bad stress"

Stress in itself is not a bad thing. It is a part of our daily lives and what can act as a motivating factor to keep us going. Smaller levels of stress are what allow us to stay focused and feel the need to reach our goals.

Excessive amounts of stress, however, is what can cause a strain on our bodies. Being exposed to too much stress will lead to mental, physical, emotional breakdowns which can lead to burn out. So don't be afraid to experience stress, but don't let stress overtake your entire life.
